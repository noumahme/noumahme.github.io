About

The point of my work is not just model performance. It is usable, well-explained progress.

I am a Computer Science PhD researcher who enjoys difficult technical problems, but I care most when the solution becomes clear, useful, and measurable. I want visitors to understand both what I do and how I think within a few seconds.

Research approach

I start with a baseline, then push for a result worth defending.

My work usually begins with a question that needs clearer evidence: can a sentence similarity model perform better, can pose estimation hold up on a harder visual domain, or can agents cooperate more reliably?

  • I compare against baselines instead of relying on vague claims.
  • I care about accuracy improvements and implementation quality equally.
  • I prefer experiments that lead to a system someone else can build on.

Builder mindset

I like turning research effort into prototypes that feel useful immediately.

That could mean engineering attack graphs from vulnerability datasets, building an LLM-based recommender for thousands of users, or programming a robotic dog to navigate autonomously.

  • I build around measurable outcomes instead of feature volume.
  • I enjoy systems that connect modeling decisions with user impact.
  • I aim for interfaces and outputs that are easy to interpret.

Teaching style

I teach with the same mindset I bring to engineering: clarity first, then depth.

Supporting students across programming, algorithms, and databases has made explanation one of my strongest technical tools.

  • I simplify hard topics without flattening the important details.
  • I enjoy creating structure that helps other people move faster.
  • I treat communication as part of strong technical work, not an extra.
